What's For Lunch

This is an exploration of food habits of Londoners, depending on their income and their area of residency. The rationale behind the project is that different social classes have different consumption patterns, hence demand different types of retail. We tried to catch these differences by looking at the name of these premises and the appearance of some specific words. We considered the business names of more than 10000 food premises across London, split them in single words and analysed the spatial distribution of these words in comparison with the distribution of household income levels. We identified fifteen tokens that were likely to have some interesting patterns and for each of them we studied the correlation with income.
